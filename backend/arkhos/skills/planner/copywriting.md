# Copywriting Skills for Landing Pages

## Headline Formula: Problem-Solution-Proof

Every landing page headline should follow the PSP framework. Lead with the outcome the visitor wants, not the product name or feature list. The hero headline must be 8 words or fewer — brevity forces clarity. Good: "Ship websites in seconds, not weeks." Bad: "Welcome to our amazing AI-powered website building platform!"

## Call-to-Action Writing

CTAs use an action verb paired with a clear benefit. Never write "Submit", "Click here", or "Learn more" — these are lazy and convert poorly. Instead: "Generate my website", "Start building free", "Download my site". The CTA should answer "what happens when I click this?" in the button text itself. Place primary CTAs above the fold and repeat them after each major content block.

## Value Propositions

Replace vague claims with specific, verifiable numbers. "Fast" becomes "17-second generation". "Affordable" becomes "EUR 0.004 per site". "Secure" becomes "GDPR-compliant, EU-hosted". Specificity builds trust; vagueness triggers skepticism. Each value prop should be a single sentence that a visitor can repeat from memory.

## Tone Guidelines for EU Brands

European brand voice is professional, trustworthy, and understated. Avoid American startup hype: no "revolutionary", no "game-changing", no exclamation marks in headlines. Confidence comes from substance, not volume. Write as if addressing a knowledgeable peer, not shouting at a crowd. French and German markets especially value precision and restraint. British markets allow slightly more warmth but still expect understatement.

## Section Copy Patterns

- Hero: Headline (8 words max) + subheadline (1 sentence expanding on the how) + CTA
- Social proof: Specific numbers ("2,400 sites generated") or recognizable logos, never "trusted by thousands"
- Features: Icon + short title (3 words) + one-sentence description per feature
- Testimonials: Real name, role, company, specific result achieved
- FAQ: Address the top 5 objections disguised as questions
