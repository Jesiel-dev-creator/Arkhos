# Creative Agency Landing Pages

## Section Order

Hero (bold statement + showreel or featured project + CTA) → Clients/Logos (recognizable brand logos) → Services (3-5 core offerings with descriptions) → Case Studies (3-4 featured projects with results) → Process (how you work: discovery, design, build, launch) → Team (key members with roles and photos) → Testimonials (client quotes with attribution) → Awards/Recognition (optional) → Contact/Brief Form → Footer.

## Copy Patterns

Agency copy should be bold, confident, and results-oriented. Lead with impact: "We build brands that outperform" not "We are a creative agency". Case study headlines should lead with the result: "3x conversion rate for Maison Laurent" not "Website redesign for Maison Laurent". Service descriptions focus on client outcomes, not internal processes. Avoid agency jargon that clients don't understand ("synergy", "holistic approach", "360-degree").

## Key Features to Include

- Featured case study with before/after or results metrics
- Service categories with clear deliverable descriptions
- Client inquiry or brief submission form
- Team section with personality (not just headshots)
- Awards or press mentions for credibility
- Blog or insights section link for thought leadership

## Design Aesthetic

Bold and asymmetric layouts that demonstrate creative capability. High contrast with one dominant accent color. Large typography as a design element — oversized headings that overlap images or break the grid. Dark backgrounds with bright accent colors work well. Display fonts should be distinctive (Clash Display, Syne, Cabinet Grotesk). Motion and interaction design should showcase technical ability — scroll-triggered animations, cursor effects, smooth page transitions.

## Photo Categories

Professional project screenshots and mockups, team working collaboratively, creative workspace environments, brand identity close-ups (business cards, packaging), event photography. All imagery should feel curated and intentional, never generic stock. Black-and-white photography with selective color accents can be striking.
