# Bakery & Patisserie Landing Pages

## Section Order

Hero (signature product photo + tagline) → About/Story (founder story, tradition, artisan craft) → Menu/Products (categorized: breads, pastries, cakes, seasonal) → Gallery (warm, inviting interior and product shots) → Location & Hours (map, opening times, phone) → Reviews/Testimonials → Contact/Order → Footer.

## Copy Patterns

Bakery copy should evoke warmth, craftsmanship, and tradition. Use sensory language: "freshly baked", "hand-crafted", "butter-rich". Mention provenance: "French flour", "local ingredients", "family recipe since 1987". Avoid corporate language — bakeries are personal. The hero headline should reference the product experience, not the business: "Artisan bread, baked at dawn" rather than "Welcome to Our Bakery".

## Key Features to Include

- Daily specials or seasonal offerings section
- Online ordering CTA (even if linking externally)
- Opening hours prominently displayed (not buried in footer)
- Address with map integration
- Menu with prices (customers expect transparency)
- Allergen information notice

## Design Aesthetic

Warm color palette: cream, soft brown, warm gold, deep burgundy accents. Photography-forward with large food images. Serif or elegant sans-serif display fonts (Playfair Display, Cormorant, Lora). Body text in warm sans-serif. Generous whitespace to let product images breathe. Subtle textures (linen, paper) can work as backgrounds but must not reduce readability.

## Photo Categories

Use food photography from Unsplash/Picsum: artisan bread close-ups, pastry displays, bakery interiors with warm lighting, flour-dusted work surfaces, coffee and croissant pairings. Avoid generic stock photos of people smiling at bread. The images should make the visitor hungry.
