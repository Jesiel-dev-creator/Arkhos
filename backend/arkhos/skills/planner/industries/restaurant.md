# Restaurant Landing Pages

## Section Order

Hero (atmospheric interior or signature dish photo + restaurant name + tagline) → About/Story (chef's philosophy, origin story, culinary tradition) → Menu (categorized: starters, mains, desserts, drinks with prices) → Gallery (food, interior, ambiance shots) → Reservation CTA (booking form or link to booking platform) → Location & Hours (map, address, parking info) → Reviews → Contact → Footer.

## Copy Patterns

Restaurant copy should evoke atmosphere and appetite. Use sensory and emotional language: "wood-fired", "hand-rolled", "slow-braised", "seasonal ingredients from local farms". The hero tagline should capture the dining experience, not describe the business: "Modern Italian, rooted in tradition" not "The best Italian restaurant in Paris". Menu descriptions should be brief (one line) but evocative, mentioning key ingredients and preparation methods.

## Key Features to Include

- Online reservation button (prominent, above the fold)
- Full menu with prices (transparency builds trust)
- Opening hours clearly visible
- Private dining or event booking information
- Dietary accommodation notices (vegetarian, gluten-free, halal)
- Chef or team section for personality

## Design Aesthetic

Warm, atmospheric palette: deep charcoal, warm cream, burgundy or olive accents for Italian; deep blue and gold for French; terracotta and sage for Mediterranean. Large, full-bleed food photography. Elegant serif display fonts (Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display) for headings. Clean sans-serif for menu items and body text. Generous spacing between menu items for readability. Subtle parallax on hero images adds depth.

## Photo Categories

Signature dishes styled beautifully, restaurant interior with warm lighting, wine glasses and table settings, kitchen action shots, ingredient close-ups (fresh herbs, olive oil, artisan bread). Lighting is crucial — warm, golden tones only. Avoid flash photography aesthetic and clinical food styling.
