# Landing Page Marketing Structure

## Optimal Section Order

The highest-converting landing pages follow this proven sequence: Hero (headline + CTA + social proof) followed by Problem (what pain does the visitor have), then Solution (how this product solves it), Features (3-6 specific capabilities), Social Proof (testimonials, logos, numbers), How It Works (3-step process), Pricing (if applicable), FAQ (objection handling), Final CTA (urgency or recap), and Footer (legal, links, contact).

## Above-the-Fold Rules

The first viewport must contain three elements: a compelling headline, a primary CTA button, and at least one trust signal (client count, rating, or recognizable logo). Nothing else is mandatory above the fold. Do not clutter it with navigation dropdowns, secondary CTAs, or feature lists. The visitor should understand the value proposition and have a clear action to take within 3 seconds of landing.

## F-Pattern and Z-Pattern Layouts

Western readers scan in an F-pattern for text-heavy pages and Z-pattern for visual pages. Place the most important information along the top horizontal bar and the left vertical bar. Headlines go top-left, CTAs go where the eye naturally rests after scanning. For image-heavy layouts (restaurants, portfolios), use the Z-pattern: logo top-left, navigation top-right, hero image center, CTA bottom-right.

## Conversion Proximity Principle

Place CTAs near trust signals. A "Start free" button converts better when it sits directly below a testimonial or next to a security badge. Never isolate a CTA in empty space — surround it with reasons to click. Repeat the primary CTA at least 3 times on a long-form landing page: after the hero, after features, and in the final section.

## Urgency and Scarcity (Honest)

EU regulations and brand trust require honest urgency. Never use fake countdown timers or fabricated scarcity. Instead, use genuine signals: "Free during beta", "3 free generations per day", or seasonal relevance. Dishonest urgency destroys trust and may violate EU consumer protection directives.
